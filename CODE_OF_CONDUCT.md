# Code of Conduct

Artifice Machine is a small workshop. We expect everyone in our spaces (issue trackers, pull requests, discussions, and any community channel we operate) to behave like guests in a craftsperson's workshop: with respect for the work, the people, and the tools.

## What we expect

**Talk about the work.** Bug reports, feature requests, design critiques, and technical disagreements are welcome. Be specific. Show your reasoning. Bring evidence when you can.

**Assume good faith.** Maintainers and contributors are usually doing their best with the time they have. If something seems wrong, ask before accusing.

**Disagree productively.** "I disagree because X" is welcome. "You are stupid for thinking X" is not.

**Respect the operator's time.** This is a small workshop. Responses may take days. That does not mean you are being ignored.

**Credit the maker.** When you build on the work, say so. When you submit a fix, your name goes in the contributors list.

## What we will not tolerate

The following are grounds for warning, comment removal, temporary suspension, or permanent ban from this organization's spaces:

- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained hostility toward any person
- Harassment of any kind, including but not limited to harassment based on race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, or physical appearance
- Doxxing, stalking, or sharing private information without consent
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Sustained disruption of conversations
- Threats of violence or harm
- Encouraging others to engage in any of the above

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it through one of these channels:

- **Email**: hello@artificemachine.com
- **GitHub**: open a private vulnerability report on the affected repository (we use this channel for sensitive non-security reports too)

Reports will be:
- Read within 3 business days
- Treated as confidential
- Investigated in good faith
- Acted upon at the operator's judgment, with the goal of keeping our spaces welcoming and productive

## Scope

This code applies to all spaces operated by Artifice Machine, including:

- All repositories under `github.com/artificemachine`
- Discussions, issue trackers, and pull request reviews on those repositories
- Official chat or community channels we run, if any
- Public events or talks given on behalf of the workshop

It does not apply to personal accounts (`@newblacc`, `@celstnblacc`) outside of work-related interactions, though we expect the same standards of conduct from the operator.

## Enforcement

The operator of Artifice Machine is the final authority on enforcement decisions in this organization's spaces. Decisions are made with the goal of maintaining a productive workshop, not with the goal of punishment.

The standard escalation:

1. **Private warning** for first offenses
2. **Public warning or temporary suspension** for repeated or serious offenses
3. **Permanent ban** for severe violations or sustained pattern of behavior

Appeals can be made by emailing hello@artificemachine.com.

## Attribution

This code is loosely inspired by the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) and the working norms of historical European craft workshops. It is intentionally short.
